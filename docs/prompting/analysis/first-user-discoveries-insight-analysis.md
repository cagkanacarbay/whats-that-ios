# Analysis

## Insight Categories

Based on the 33 discoveries, insights fall into these categories:

### 1. Historical Facts / Dates
- "Built in 1248" / "Founded in 1236" / "Opened in 1874"
- "Construction began in 1386"
- "In April 1609, Henry Hudson set sail..."

### 2. Origin Stories
- How something came to be named (Boston named after Boston Stump)
- How a tradition started (press-ganged sailor becomes naval hero)
- Why something was built (to compete with St. Peter's in Rome)

### 3. Hidden Mechanics / How Things Work
- Big Ben is tuned with pennies (two-fifths of a second per day)
- Makuti roofs create natural air conditioning through convection
- Dartboard fibers "self-heal" by springing back
- Hussar wing feathers created terrifying sound in wind
- Flying buttresses push weight outward to allow glass walls

### 4. Surprising Connections
- This tower is why New York exists (Hudson sailed from Schreierstoren)
- This church is why Boston, Massachusetts has its name
- Napoleon stole the Brandenburg Gate chariot for 8 years
- A-U-F became slang for "free" because of cathedral tax exemptions

### 5. Scale / Numbers That Stick
- 1,300 rooms in the Palace of Knossos
- 3,400 statues on Milan's Duomo
- 660 generals' names inside the Arc de Triomphe
- 100,000 names in Edinburgh's memorial books
- 30 kilograms of hussar armor weight
- 16,500 kg equestrian statue weight
- 272 feet tall (Boston Stump)
- 9 meters across (Zürich clock dial - largest in Europe)

### 6. Legends / Myths
- Ivan the Terrible blinded the builders (probably false)
- Hezarfen flew across the Bosphorus with wooden wings (debated)
- "Tower of Tears" name is actually about a sharp angle, not weeping wives

### 7. Craft / Material Insights
- Coral stone "breathes" in humid climates
- Upside-down tree trunks prevent sprouting (Knossos columns)
- Coconut fiber rope allows buildings to flex in storms
- Mortarless stone walls shift safely during earthquakes

### 8. Cultural Context
- "Pole pole" (slowly) as Zanzibar philosophy

1. **Big Ben is tuned with pennies** - Adding one penny changes time by 0.4 seconds per day
2. **Boston, Massachusetts is named after the Boston Stump** - Vicar John Cotton's followers fled and named their city
3. **Napoleon stole the Brandenburg Gate chariot** - Kept it in Paris for 8 years
4. **Henry Hudson sailed from Amsterdam** - The Schreierstoren is why New York exists
5. **Hussar wings made terrifying sounds** - Feathers vibrated to break enemy morale
6. **A-U-F became slang for "free"** - From tax-exempt cathedral marble boats
7. **Ivan the Terrible blinding the builders is probably a myth**
8. **The "Tower of Tears" name is wrong** - It means "sharp angle" not weeping
9. **Knossos columns are upside-down trees** - Placed that way to prevent sprouting
10. **Milan cathedral only finished in 1965** - After 600 years
11. **No building in Milan could be taller than the Madonnina**
12. **The Order of Malta issues passports and stamps** - Sovereign state with no land
13. **Nijubashi means "Double Bridge"** - Had to stack two bridges because moat was too deep
14. **Cologne crane sat unfinished for 300 years** - Became symbol of abandoned dreams
15. **St. Basil's is actually 9 separate chapels**
