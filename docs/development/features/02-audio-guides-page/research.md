# Audio Guides Page – Research

## User Profile & Context
- **Tourist, heads-up, on-foot.** Moving through a place while creating discoveries and listening to the narration as each one becomes ready; phone often pocketed, earbuds in.
- **Example – Prague Old Town:** Arrives at the square, snaps the Astronomical Clock, moves a block to the Church of Our Lady before Týn, then heads to St. Vitus Cathedral and across Charles Bridge. Photos are taken back-to-back; voiceovers generate while the tourist keeps walking and listening.
- **Example – Art museum:** Enters a gallery at the National Gallery, captures three paintings in a row, walks to the next room while current audio plays. Wants to hear each new voiceover as it finishes without stopping the current track; may pause briefly to replay a favorite piece.

## Core Needs (tourist-on-the-move)
- **Flow certainty:** Know that new voiceovers are being created in the background without interrupting current playback; “it’s in progress” vs. “it’s ready” is obvious.
- **Awareness without interruption:** Lightweight signal when a just-captured discovery becomes ready so the tourist can choose to switch—or finish the current narration first.
- **Control over what’s next:** A simple, trustable order for the last few captures (e.g., the three they just took), with the ability to advance to the next ready one without losing position.
- **Recovery and retries:** If generation fails, it’s visible and fixable later with a quick retry—no dead ends or guesswork.
- **Clarity on readiness:** At-a-glance separation of “ready to play” vs. “still generating,” so they don’t keep tapping items that aren’t available yet.
- **Minimal decision friction:** One-tap actions for the field: keep listening, jump to the next ready item, or queue a newly ready item—nothing more.

## Implications for the Audio Guides Page
- Show just-created items with live status (processing → ready) alongside the current/queued playback.
- Surface “Ready to play” without auto-snatching focus; give explicit “Play now / Play next” choices.
- Keep an “Up Next” view scoped to the recent captures so batch sessions (e.g., 3 back-to-back photos) are easy to follow.
- Allow quick retry on failed/missing items, but keep it lightweight so listening is uninterrupted.
