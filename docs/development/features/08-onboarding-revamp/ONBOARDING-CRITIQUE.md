# Onboarding Critique: The 40-45% Drop-Off Problem

## The Core Issue

Your onboarding has a **feature-telling problem**, not a **benefit-showing problem**. Let me break down what I see:

---

## Current Flow Analysis

### PRE-ONBOARDING (before sign-up):

**Slide 1**
- **Copy:** "See the world with new eyes" / "Point your camera and let the world share its stories."
- **Verdict:** Vague promise. No concrete benefit. What stories? Why should I care?

**Slide 2**
- **Copy:** "Stories tailored to you" / "Answers adapt to your interests and get smarter with every photo."
- **Verdict:** Feature-focused ("adapts," "smarter"). Still no taste of magic.

---

### POST-ONBOARDING (after sign-up):

**Slide 1**
- **Copy:** "Welcome aboard! You've got 3 free credits..."
- **Problem:** Immediately introduces friction (credits = scarcity). First thing after signing up is a limitation?

**Slide 2**
- **Copy:** "Snap and get answers" / "instant explanations tailored to what you care about"
- **Problem:** Still telling, not showing. Generic.

**Slide 3**
- **Copy:** Voice picker
- **Problem:** Useful but not magical

**Slide 4**
- **Copy:** Content preferences (IPOP)
- **Problem:** Blocking requirement before they've seen ANY value

**Slide 5**
- **Copy:** Location permission
- **Problem:** Necessary but feels like another hoop

**Slide 6**
- **Copy:** "Make your first discovery"
- **Problem:** Finally the CTA, but by now they've been through 6 slides of setup

---

## The Brutal Truth

A new user installs your app curious and excited. Your onboarding then:

1. Shows them pretty-but-abstract art
2. Tells them about features in generic terms
3. Immediately mentions credit limitations
4. Forces them to configure preferences before they've experienced any value
5. Asks for location permission
6. Finally lets them try the app

**They've done 6 steps of work and received zero magic.**

The benefits you listed from your website are excellent:
- See what others miss
- Get inside the minds of the past
- Know what actually happened
- Hear the local stories
- Feel the history of where you're standing

**But none of these appear in your onboarding.**

---

## The Problem with Your Images

The artwork is beautiful but abstract. A woman with sunglasses reflecting scenery, a man looking at stars, a person juggling oranges...

**None of these show the app doing its magic.**

Hopkins would say: "Pictures should not be used merely because they are interesting. Or to attract attention. Or to decorate an ad."

**What if a user saw an actual discovery?** A photo someone took, and the incredible story that came back? That's the magic. That's what makes someone think "I want that."

---

## Recommended Approach: Show The Magic First

**Principle:** Don't make them configure before they've tasted the value.

Here's a restructured flow:

### Pre-Onboarding (2 slides) — Show the Magic

**Slide 1: The Hook**
- **Image:** An actual photo someone took (famous landmark, interesting building, artwork)
- **Title:** "What's the story behind this?"
- **Message:** Show a snippet of an actual discovery response — the kind of surprising, vivid story your app generates
- **Purpose:** Immediately demonstrate the magic

**Slide 2: The Benefit Stack**
- **Image:** Collage or split-screen showing diverse use cases (landmark, food, artwork, sign)
- **Title:** "Turn any photo into its story"
- **Message:** Quick benefit bullets:
  - "Hear stories others miss"
  - "Know what actually happened"
  - "Listen while you walk"
- **CTA:** "Get Started"

---

### Post-Onboarding — Get Them to Magic Fast

**Slide 1: Welcome + Let Them Try Immediately**
- Skip the credit talk. Just say "Welcome to What's That?"
- Immediate CTA: "Take your first photo" or "Try with one of ours"
- Offer a demo mode — let them photograph something RIGHT NOW or use a sample image to see the magic

**Slide 2: Voice Selection**
- Keep it, but make it quick
- Consider: do they need to choose voice before their first discovery? Can it default?

**Slide 3: After Their First Discovery — Ask for Preferences**

Now they've seen the magic. NOW ask them:
- "Want more stories like this? Tell us what you care about most."
- IPOP makes sense only after they understand what they're getting

**Slide 4: Location Permission (if needed)**
- Frame it as unlocking more: "Enable location to get stories about exactly where you're standing"

### Revised Onboarding Proposal

#### PRE-ONBOARDING (2 slides — before sign-up)

**Slide 1: Show the Magic**
- **Visual:** Use one of the actual app screenshots (Klimt or Zagreb roof — visually striking + title/story visible)
- **Title:** "This is what you've been walking past."
- **Message:** "One photo. A story you'll never forget."
- **Buttons:** Skip / Next

**Why this works:** Immediate proof. They see exactly what the app produces. The title creates tension ("what have I been missing?").

**Slide 2: The Benefit Stack**
- **Visual:** A grid/collage of 3-4 discovery screenshots (showing variety: art, architecture, artifact, landmark)
- **Title:** "Turn any photo into its story."
- **Message:** Three quick benefit bullets:
  - • See what others miss
  - • Hear stories, not facts
  - • Listen while you walk
- **Button:** Get Started

**Why this works:** Shows breadth (works on anything), delivers your website benefits in punchy form, leads naturally to sign-up.

---

#### POST-ONBOARDING (Simplified — after sign-up)

**Slide 1: Welcome + Immediate Action**
- **Visual:** Clean, minimal — maybe the app icon or a simple illustration
- **Title:** "Welcome to What's That?"
- **Message:** "You've got 3 free discoveries. Let's make your first one."
- **Buttons:** "Take a Photo" (primary) / "Use Gallery" (secondary)

**Why this works:**
- Mentions credits without making it feel like a limitation (it's an invitation, not a restriction)
- Gets them to the magic immediately
- No configuration required yet

**Slide 2: Voice Selection (AFTER first discovery)**

Show this after they've made their first discovery, when they're already hooked.
- **Title:** "Choose your narrator."
- **Message:** "Pick a voice for your audio guides. You can always change this later."

**Slide 3: Preferences (AFTER first discovery)**

Now they understand what they're personalizing.
- **Title:** "Get more of what you love."
- **Message:** "Tell us what matters most — we'll shape your stories around it."

**Location Permission:**

This can be a soft prompt within the app when relevant (e.g., when they try to use location-based features), rather than a mandatory onboarding slide.
